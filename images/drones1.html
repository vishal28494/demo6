Drone Infrastructure in NJIT

Aim


The New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT) – in collaboration with IEEE North Jersey Section – announces Drones for Infrastructure 2019, a technical workshop on the design, implementation, applications and societal implications of using drones and other unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs).

The workshop is intended for individuals with scientific, technical, policy, and implementation interests in drones and UAVs. Sessions will be available on a wide range of issues from navigation algorithms for cohorts of coordinated drones to the laws ethics of drone-use in inspection in the public arena. Researchers and administrators in these areas are encouraged to submit proposals for special sessions, nominations of keynote speakers, proposals for live demonstrations, and papers for oral and poster presentations.

Workshops


The workshop will take place on the NJIT Campus, (University Heights, Newark, NJ 07102)

Call For Papers


Authors are invited to submit abstracts describing theoretical advances, applications, and ideas in all fields related for the use of drones for infrastructure. Authors of selected abstracts will be invited to submit full papers for peer review. See the table below for the timeline.

Abstracts (due 14 September 2018): The body of the abstract should be limited to 500 words and should include the title, name(s) of author(s) and author affiliation(s). The abstract should provide clear description of the planned contribution with a short summary of the main findings/conclusions.

Full Papers for review (due 16 November 2018): Full papers are intended to report original work not previously published elsewhere. They should address topics relevant to the theme of the workshop and state clearly the contribution and findings of the reported work in the appropriate context.

Final submissions for inclusion in the proceedings (due 1 September 2018): the final paper submission should address reviewer comments (if any) and follow the standard IEEE conference template (double-column format). A manuscript template available at: https://www.ieee.org/conferences/publishing/templates.html. Full papers will be limited to 6 pages (a $150/page additional charge will be made for pages 6-10). Up to 6 Index terms or Keywords can be used. to submit a proposal for an invited session

Presentations: accepted papers will be allotted 20 minutes for an oral presentation (+5 minutes Q/A) or assigned to a poster presentation. All accepted papers will be reproduced in the conference proceedings, and published in IEEE Xplore. Inclusion of an accepted paper in the conference proceedings and in IEEE Xplore is requires a full registration of at least one author, and the actual presentation of the paper in the conference by one of its authors.

All papers should be submitted via EDAS using this link (http://edas.info/N25125). Authors will be asked to use an existing EDAS account or create a new one if they did not use EDAS before.
