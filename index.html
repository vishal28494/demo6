IEEE Drones For Infrastructure
A Technical Workshop
(NJIT, Newark, NJ - March 19-22, 2019)


The New Jersey Institute of Technology (NJIT) – in collaboration with IEEE North Jersey Section – announces Drones for Infrastructure 2019, a technical workshop on the design, implementation, applications and societal implications of using drones and unmanned aerial systems (UAS).

The workshop is intended for individuals with scientific, technical, policy, and implementation interests in drones and UAS. Sessions will be available on a wide range of issues, from navigation algorithms for cohorts of coordinated drones, to the regulations and ethics of drone use. Researchers and administrators with interest in these areas are encouraged to submit proposals for special sessions, proposals for live demonstrations, and papers for oral and poster presentations at the workshop.


Workshop Dates: March 19-22, 2019


The workshop will take place on the NJIT Campus, (University Heights, Newark, NJ 07102)
Please email us at drones@njit.edu for general information queries.

Focus Areas And Topics



Design and Implementation


● Flight control, navigation, telematics, and collision avoidance

● Autonomous drones

● Drone formation flying and swarming

● Underwater drone technology

● Computing aspects of drones

● Drone communication and communication security.

● Ethics of drone manufacturing, design and use

Regulation, Education And Training, Usage in Popular Culture


● Use of drones in education

● Training for drone operators

● Regulation and legal framework for registration, licensing and operation of drones

● Public safety and security aspects of drone operation

● Drones in the popular press, movies, internet and other public media

● Real estate, insurance, tourism, and entertainment

Applications of Drones


● Infrastructure inspection (e.g., bridges, tunnels, dams, pipelines, structures, industrial plants)

● Surveying (pre-construction and during construction)

● Traffic surveillance (data collection) and traffic incident management

● Utilities management

● Oil and gas industry

● Maritime applications, including underwater

● Mail and parcel delivery

● Homeland security, law enforcement

● 5G Communication

● Remote sensing, mapping, surveying, SLAM, and aerial data collection

● Meteorology, climate and weather prediction and analysis

● Disaster response and post-disaster response

● Agricultural and farming




Call For Papers


Authors are invited to submit abstracts describing theoretical advances, applications, and ideas for advancing the use of drones related to the workshop focus areas and topics. Authors of selected abstracts will be invited to submit full papers for peer review. The timeline for abstract and paper submission and review is the following:

Abstracts (due 28 September 2018):The body of the abstract should be limited to 500 words and should include the title, name(s) of author(s) and author affiliation(s). The abstract should provide clear description of the planned contribution with a short summary of the main findings/conclusions.

Full Papers submission for review (due 16 November 2018): Full papers are intended to report original work not previously published elsewhere. They should address topics relevant to the theme of the workshop and state clearly the contribution and findings of the reported work in the appropriate context.

Final paper submission for inclusion in the workshop proceedings (due 1 February 2019): The final paper submission should address reviewer comments (if any) and follow the standard IEEE conference template (double-column format). A manuscript template is available at: https://www.ieee.org/conferences/publishing/templates.html. Full papers will be limited to 6 pages (a $150/page additional charge will be made for pages 6-10). Up to 6 Index Terms or Keywords can be used.

Presentations: accepted papers will be allotted 20 minutes for an oral presentation (+5 minutes Q/A) or assigned to a poster presentation. All accepted papers will be reproduced in the conference proceedings, and published in IEEE Xplore. Inclusion of an accepted paper in the conference proceedings and in IEEE Xplore requires a full registration of at least one author, and the actual presentation of the paper in the conference by one of its authors.

All papers should be submitted via EDAS using this link (http://edas.info/N25125 [edas.info]). Authors will be asked to use an existing EDAS account or create a new one if they did not use EDAS before.

Call for Invited Sessions


Individuals interested in organizing an Invited (Special) Session are kindly asked to submit a proposal (see below) by 28 September 2018. The proposals should identify the theme of the session and the invited authors/presenters (with tentative titles of the planned papers).

Organizers will be notified of acceptance no later than 12 October 2018, along with instructions for paper submission by the authors. Final manuscripts will be due by 1 February 2019.

Presentations: each session paper will be allotted 20 minutes for an oral presentation (+5 minutes Q/A). All session papers will be reproduced in the conference proceedings, and published in IEEE Xplore. Inclusion of an accepted paper in the conference proceedings and in IEEE Xplore requires a full registration of at least one author, and the actual presentation of the paper in the conference by one of its authors.

Call for Demonstrations


Individuals and organizations are invited to submit proposals for live demonstrations of drone operation. The purpose of these demonstrations is to showcase state-of-the-art drone technology and solutions, and their practical application for different use cases. For more details, please download and review the Detailed Call for Demonstrations.

Demonstration proposals are due on 28 September 2018 and applicants will be notified of acceptance no later than 12 October 2018. The proposal for live demonstration should be submitted by filling out the Demonstration Application Form. The demonstration abstract should be included in the application form (in the appropriate text box), and should provide a description of the type of application or technology to be demonstrated. The application should also describe the logistical and technical aspects of the proposed demonstration.

Please fill out and submit online the Demonstration Application Form, if you are interested in performing a live drone demonstration at the workshop.
